# ISO 42001

## ❓ What is it?

ISO 42001 is an international standard for building and governing an Artificial Intelligence Management System.

---

| 🔍 What does it do? | 🧠 Why do we need it? | ⚡ Why now? |
|--------------------|----------------------|------------|
| It provides a structured framework for how organizations develop, deploy, monitor, and govern AI systems across their lifecycle. | The standard was created because AI is now being used in high-risk business areas where poor oversight can lead to privacy violations, biased decisions, inaccurate outputs, legal exposure, regulatory scrutiny, and loss of public trust. | It matters now because many organizations are adopting AI quickly, but their governance, documentation, and risk controls have not kept pace with the speed of AI adoption. |

---

AI risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks that arise from the use of AI systems within an organization.

**Identify → Assess → Mitigate**

AI risk management and governance in practice means having clear processes, controls, and accountability for how AI systems are selected, used, and monitored within an organization. Without proper oversight, AI systems can cause harm such as biased decision-making, data misuse, or legal and regulatory violations. For example, Amazon discontinued an AI recruiting tool after it was found to be biased against women, demonstrating how ungoverned AI can create both ethical and legal risk for organizations.
